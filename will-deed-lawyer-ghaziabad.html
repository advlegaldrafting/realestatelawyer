Q: What is a Will Deed?

A: Will Deed is a legal document through which a person (testator) declares how their assets and property will be distributed after their death.

Q: Is registration of a Will mandatory in India?

A: No, registration of a Will is not mandatory under the Indian Registration Act, 1908. However, it is advisable as it adds authenticity and reduces the chances of disputes.

Q: Where can a Will be registered?

A: A Will can be registered at the office of the Sub-Registrar within whose jurisdiction the testator resides or where the property is located.

Q: Who can make a Will?

A: Any person who is of sound mind and above 18 years of age can make a Will.

Q: What documents are required for Will registration?

A: Original Will document,Identity proof of the testator (Aadhaar, PAN, etc.),Address proof,Passport-size photographs,Details of two witnesses,Identity proofs of witnesses.

Q: What is the process for registering a Will?

A: Draft the Will clearly mentioning all assets and beneficiaries,Sign the Will in the presence of two witnesses,Visit the Sub-Registrar office with all required documents,Pay the applicable registration fee,The Registrar verifies the identity and records the Will,The registered Will is safely kept and a copy may be provided.

Q: Is stamp duty required on a Will?

A: No, stamp duty is not applicable on a Will in India. Only a nominal registration fee is charged.

Q: Can a Will be changed after registration?

A: Yes, a Will can be modified or revoked anytime by the testator during their lifetime by creating a new Will.

Q: What happens after the death of the testator?

A: After the testator’s death, the Will comes into effect. In some cases, beneficiaries may need to obtain probate from the court.
